## Anti-fabrication guardrails (why-this-team-side)

- **Do not invent traction, customer counts, or growth rates** in any of these framings. The closest you can come is "shipped nine public AI products before CiteForge" (verified — including Vettd at vettd-app.com and InfinityHire at infinityhire.ai) and "five peer-reviewed papers grounding the product" (verified). Numbers like MRR, customers, design partners, and citation lift are `[FILL: …]` placeholders managed by the founder.
- **Do not claim a co-founder.** CiteForge is solo-founder today. If a form asks for "founders (plural)", the honest answer is "1 today, [FILL: hiring plan]."
- **Do not claim YC alums or notable investors as advisors.** The skill has no such facts. If they exist, the founder will add them to `founder-bio.md`'s Recognition section first.
- **Do not over-claim the M.Tech.** It is *in progress*, not completed. Always say "pursuing" or "currently completing", never "completed" or "graduated".
- **Do not call the team "we" if writing from the founder's first-person voice when honesty matters more than scale.** "I have spent 14 years…" is fine for solo cold emails. "We have spent 14 years…" reads as a team and should only be used in deck / About / web copy where the IntelliForge studio framing is established.
